3-1.Understand the terms: (a) stress; (b) strain; What is the engineering
stress and engineering strain? why we say engineering stress and engineering
strain instead of ‘true stress and true strain’?

Stress is defined as a resistance offered by a material. It is a reaction of
a material to external force.

Strain is change in size or shape of a material which subjects to external
force.

Engineering stress is the quotient of average uniaxial tensile force to the
original cross-sectional area.

Engineering strain is calculated by the change in sample length divided by
the original length of the sample.

That is because in reality the cross-section of the material tends to shrink
during the pulling process or expend during the compression. So the actual
stress should be calculated by divided force with the true cross-section area
‘Ai’. And the true strain is calculated by Napierian logarithm li/l0, where li
is the final length.

因为在实际过程中，材料的横截面在外界拉力或压力的作用下，会减少或增大。因此，真实应力应该由外力除以最终的横截面积得到。而真实应变应该由自然对数ln（li/l0）计算。其中li是材料最终的长度。
